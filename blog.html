Because Inertia isn't a companion app.

"Companion apps help you remember. Inertia helps you understand."

Discovery AND Validation

Inertia serves two complementary purposes:

Discovery (Exploration & Testing)

The fundamental loop of learning through experimentation:

Test ‚Üí Discover ‚Üí Understand ‚Üí Iterate

Inertia reveals the hidden physics variables you can actually control‚Äîlaunch angle, RPM, spin decay, combo interactions. You're not just battling, you're discovering what your combos can do, learning why they behave the way they do, testing what's possible.

This is the scientific method in practice. That "wait, what?" moment when invisible physics becomes obvious.

Validation ("Prove It" Infrastructure)

The Beyblade community has a problem: balance debates run on limited data instead of hard evidence.

Inertia provides the data infrastructure that makes informed community decisions possible:

  • ‚ÜíOrganizers can experiment with formats and track outcomes
  • ‚ÜíPlayers can prove what actually works with data, not just tier lists
  • ‚ÜíCommunities can make evidence-based restriction decisions
  • ‚ÜíForum arguments backed by evidence instead of vibes

October 2025 - Momentum Campaign Launch

Public demonstrations of Insight and Axis prototypes. Community reception and what's next.

The Momentum Campaign

After months of development in private, we publicly demonstrated working prototypes of Insight (precision launch analyzer) and Axis (computer vision tournament system) in October 2025.

This wasn't vaporware or concept renders. These were functional prototypes solving real problems for competitive players and tournament organizers.

What We Showed

Insight Prototype

Real-time RPM tracking, launch angle measurement (IMU), and Bluetooth sync to the Inertia App. Compact attachment form factor with live LCD feedback.

Real-time battle detection, win classification (Spin Finish, KO, Burst), and automated clip generation. Tournament-ready accuracy for organizers.

Building the Right Way

We're not rushing hardware to market. Foundation before features. Proof before hype.

The Inertia App launches free in Q1 2026 because tournament infrastructure needs to be solid before hardware integrates with it. Insight and Axis follow when they're production-ready, not when arbitrary deadlines demand it.

This approach takes longer. But competitive players deserve tools that respect the craft‚Äînot rushed products that overpromise and underdeliver.
