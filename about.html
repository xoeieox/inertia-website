ABOUT INERTIA

Building data-driven performance analysis for competitive Beyblade

The community deserves tools as sophisticated as their dedication.

The Mission

Competitive Beyblade players invest hundreds of hours perfecting their technique, customizing builds, and competing at tournaments. But there's a gap between the community's dedication and the tools available to measure performance.

Inertia exists to close that gap. We're building an integrated ecosystem—hardware and software—that provides objective, data-driven insights into launch quality, battle outcomes, and tournament performance.

"Prove it's a sport. One data point at a time."

The Problem We're Solving

The Gap

  • • Competitive players train with no objective feedback on launch quality
  • • Tournament organizers spend 90+ minutes manually managing brackets
  • • Battle outcomes are recorded on paper or not tracked at all
  • • No way to prove skill vs. luck without longitudinal data

The Solution

  • ✓ Insight: Launch analyzer that measures RPM, angle, and consistency
  • ✓ Axis: Computer vision system for automated battle recording
  • ✓ Inertia App: Tournament platform that connects everything
  • ✓ Data Integration: Launch metrics + battle outcomes + tournament history

The Founder

◈

Built by a Competitive Player

Inertia is built by someone who understands the gap between competitive dedication and available tooling—because I've lived it.

My background is in photography and visual systems. I've spent years building precision tools for capturing and analyzing performance. When I got serious about competitive Beyblade, I realized the same principles applied: elite performance requires objective feedback.

Inertia is what I wish existed when I started competing. Tools that respect the craft. Data that proves skill development. A platform that treats battling tops like the sport it is.
