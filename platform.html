The Vision

Competitive Beyblade is a sport. Players train for hours, organizers run structured tournaments, and communities build around competition. But without objective data, it's hard to discover what actually works, prove skill development, or enable informed community decisions.

Inertia solves this with an integrated platform that reveals the physics variables you can actually control. Launch analytics, battle verification, and tournament infrastructure—each product enables discovery AND validation. Together, they help you understand WHY a combo won, not just that it won.

"Discovery at the individual level. Validation at the community level. Both happening simultaneously."

Insight Enables Discovery

When you use Insight, every launch syncs to your app profile. RPM, angle, consistency—revealing the physics variables you can actually control. Test → Discover → Understand → Iterate. Practice sessions build a longitudinal dataset of your technique development.

What this enables:

  • Discover how YOUR launch technique affects performance
  • Track improvements and breakthrough moments over time
  • See how consistent you are under tournament pressure
  • When everyone's testing and sharing, patterns emerge that no individual could find

Axis Captures Battle Data

When organizers use Axis, battle outcomes auto-populate in the app. Computer vision verifies wins. Automated clips are generated. Tournament footage becomes part of your match history.

What this enables:

  • Review verified footage of your tournament battles
  • Analyze opponent patterns and movement strategies
  • Build a portfolio of tournament performance

Why Integration Matters

Standalone tools are useful. A launch analyzer is great. A tournament app saves time. Computer vision is impressive.

But integrated systems unlock exponential value. Launch data means more when it correlates with battle outcomes. Tournament brackets are richer with verified footage. Performance analytics gain context from longitudinal tracking.

This is why Inertia is a platform, not a collection of products. Everything connects. Data flows between hardware and software. The whole becomes greater than the sum of its parts.
